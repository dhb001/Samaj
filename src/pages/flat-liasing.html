Our Flat Liasing Journey

OUR HISTORY

When the pioneers of Shree Cutchi Leva Patel Samaj, Nairobi, established Samaj as a Society, one of the objects in the Constitution was to acquire, erect, and maintain movable and immovable properties within Kenya to deserving members at subsidized rates.

For this reason, on 24 November 1996, the pioneers' vision of constructing a residential estate was accomplished when the foundation stone was laid on a 5-acre piece of land located at Nairobi West.

During a period of 17 years, i.e., from 1996 – 2013, the Samaj Management made it possible for home ownership to be no longer a dream for the Community members as over 500 residential flats have been constructed at Nairobi West and Langata within Nairobi County and handed to the new owners as follows: –

Committee Structure

COMMITTEE FORMATION

To ease flat management, a Flat Liaison Sub-Committee was formed, comprising a Convenor and 5 members whose responsibility is to take charge of all Rental Properties.

MAIN DUTIES AND RESPONSIBILITIES

  • Receive, scrutinize, and shortlist rental application forms and conduct interviews for all the shortlisted rental applicants.
  • Ensure that during vacancy, the rental flats are painted and repair work done to an acceptable standard in readiness for occupancy by the new tenant.
  • Ensure rent payments are made on time by following up with rental defaulters.
  • During the transfer of home ownership, review the sale/purchase application and recommend approval of the sale to the Samaj Management Committee.
  • In collaboration with the Samaj Caretaker to inspect, repair, and execute any works that fall under the Landlord's obligation
  • Ensure all the Samaj rental and guest premises are maintained in good condition.
  • Approve the Rental Deposit Refund upon vacating the rental premises.
